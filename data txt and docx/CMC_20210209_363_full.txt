This letter was written by a person incarcerated at CMC (California Men's Colony).</br></br>
Sorry, my handwriting is not very good. I did not have the privilege of studying. I cannot write in English, only in Spanish. I hope you understand me. Well, being here in prison is depression, stress. This pandemic, this for me is very difficult. Day after day, it is very difficult. Not only for me but for everyone as much as in prison.</br></br>As out there, here, I did get infected with COVID-19. They are very strong symptoms. Sometimes I came to think the worst about my life in what was going to happen with me. At that time, my family, my mother, my father, my brothers are 10 years that I have not seen. When they told me I was infected with COVID-19, all I thought was to see my family one more time.</br></br> 

I did not know about my family for five years. They did not want to know about me for the crime I committed. I did not value my life or anyone else's, but when you go through something is when you open your eyes and realize the value of a person's life. Color does not matter, race, we all have the right to life. Not envying nobody, nor do I have a grudge.</br></br>To the contrary, with my heart, I ask everyone to please take care of yourselves. Life is very valuable. I do my best to take care of myself this time. I have already come out of this pandemic. If I do not take care of myself, I can become infected with COVID-19. I have been exercising for 10 years and I think that helps me to overcome illnesses. It keeps me calmer. I think more clearly about the problems about COVID-19.</br></br>Take care of yourselves as much as possible. I am glad to have received your letters. Again, I say goodbye, blessings to the whole team.</br></br>
